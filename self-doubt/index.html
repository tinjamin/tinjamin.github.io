Self Doubt

March 17, 2022
Author: Tinjamin
Tags: [Life]

Why I’m Even Writing

It is currently Thursday 11:33am, mid-day with classes still going on, yet I, a try-hard, have opted to write. Funny enough, this morning I took my second Calc II Exam and one of the worse things that could possibly have happened, happened. Please understand worse as in the academic, student kind (which, I know completely ruins everything that I’m about to write, but please allow me to indulge myself as I sit in a pool of complete bitterness). Away from that tangent, allow me to retell the events of this morning.

Everything went perfectly as planned during the exam. I even had 30 extra minutes to check over my test, that’s a lot of time for an exam, so that’s what I did. I checked it once, and then I checked it twice. As the time began to dwindle, I crumpled to the pressure. I can perfectly recall my heart racing, my legs shaking, and my hands sweating. I was mindful of my growing anxiety; however, in just the last 30 seconds, something happened that made me lose that mindfulness, which led me to erase my answers even though it was right.

In the Moment

Just moments after the exam, I was able to regain my mindfulness, and then it struck me: I had just erased the right answers. Can you imagine the thoughts racing through my mind and the self-hatred that was coursing through my veins. I had checked it over and over again, yet in the last 30ish seconds, I threw it all away due to a simple miscalculation that I now presume stemmed from my anxiety and my inability to recognize it. I was throwing a tantrum, a volcanic eruption of emotions as if the world was going to collapse any second from now.

What I’ve Learned

Despite the tragic event occurring just a few hours ago, I have come to learn a few things from this unfortunate incident. First, the bitterness of self-doubt. I have learned to have more confidence in myself and my findings. The possible danger of this confidence is the pin-pointing of one’s perspective leading to a narrow-minded point of view. The way to oppose this is through fostering investigative confidence, a term I’ve just made up. Investigative confidence refers to confidence that is backed up by a solid foundation constructed through investigation, experimentation, and discussion. It is not simply confidence that stems from a perception, which is often incorrect, of correctness, rather confidence that is supported and sits upon a solid foundation.

Second, the beauty of mindfulness. The peace and calmness that I had throughout the majority of the exam stemmed from preparation and, more importantly, mindfulness. I was aware of my anxiety, my fears, and my flaws. I had a good grasp on myself and my thoughts. The mindfulness brought me clarity and helped me access the state of flow. As the time dwindled, my ability to remain mindful did as well. This, I believe, to be the true root cause of the tragic incident. Had I remained calm and mindful, then I would have seen the mistake that I was making; however, I lost all sense of mindfulness and fully gave in to the anxiety, stress, and pressure. This is precisely why just few moments after submitting the exam that I regained by mindfulness and re-awake to reality rather than succumbing to stress and anxiety that I comprehend my actions. Without colorizing and romanticising the experience, during the last few moments of the exam, I truly lost all sense of thinking and let myself become devoured by chaos. With that being said, this experience is something that deep down I’m grateful for because it has taught me the essence of mindfulness and the beauty of simply and truly being present.
