The Beginning

I’ve never written for myself. I’ve only written for others. The sequences of words carefully placed to portray a facade of depth, only to have a shallow, hallow inside. It’s this staleness that inspired me to create this blog. I wanted a platform that focused on three things: discomfort, wisdom, and love.

"Seek Discomfort. Seek Wisdom. Seek Love."

Seek Discomfort.

For the unfortunate few who are unaware, this is the heart and pride of Yes Theory. The channel started with a group of college friends who wanted to take on the task of saying “Yes” to some of the randomest and wildest challenges they receive. Starting with completing an item a day on their bucket list for 30 days, this group of friends gave birth to a channel, more importantly, a family that is comprised of 7.28 million and spread across several nations.

I discover their channel when I was a freshman or sophomore in highschool. With the stress from school and the lack of time to pursue things that I really wanted to do, I turned to Yes Theory to imagine what life would be like if I started to say “Yes”. Perhaps, it was my discovery of Yes Theory that makes many people I know today perceive me as idealistic and a dreamer. Oddly enough, I have grown to embrace such perception for being idealistic brings forth color in a dull, pragmatic world and being a dreamer unbounds you from the binding shackles of society.

Since then, my “if I started to say ‘Yes’” has slightly transformed to “I must say ‘Yes’”. Indeed, being a college student doesn’t help much with regards to time and stress, but I have learned that those two things will never go away. I have become a seeker of discomfort, albeit an inexperienced and slightly fearful one, but I am still nonetheless a seeker. The seek for discomfort lies in the brief moments of daily life: a decision to pursue a spark, an ignition of spontaneity, a “Yes” to the things that make you uncomfortable.

Seek Wisdom.

All that needs to be said for these two words are:

"An intelligent man may know many things, but a wise man knows what to do with them."

The seek for wisdom is more than just reading books, quoting philosophical phrases, and reordering verbs and nouns in every confusing way to sound intellectual. The seek for wisdom is rooted in the desire to find the truth and every bit of understanding gained towards such truth is the attainment of a bit more wisdom.

Seek Love.

I don’t think much need to be said here as well (this is the benefit of choosing cliche phrases as your slogan ;). Just as the “unexamined life is not worth living” so is the lonely life. No matter how introverted one may be, life is a shared experience, and you always need someone to experience it with. Whether it’s your significant other, your family, your friends, or even your pet: love, as Erich Fromm puts it, “is union with somebody, or something, outside oneself, under the condition of retaining the separateness and integrity of one’s own self”.
