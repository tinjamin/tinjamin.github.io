Definition

An alternating series is a series whose terms alternate between positive and negative values, often taking one of the two following forms:

$$ \sum (-1)^{n-1}b_n \qquad \text{ or } \qquad \sum (-1)^n b_n $$

where $ b_n > 0 $

Alternating Series Test

If the alternating series satisfy both of the following conditions:

  • terms are decreasing where $ b_{n+1} < b_n $ for eventually all $ n $
  • terms going to zero: $ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} b_n = 0 $

then the series is convergent, else the test is inconclusive.

Intuition

The first condition makes sure that each following term decreases and with $ b_n $ approaching $ 0 $, it means that the addition or substraction of each successive term is less than the absolute value of the previous term and getting closer to $ 0 $; therefore, the partial sum approaches the actual partial sum value.

Remark

  • Turn the term into a function and use the derivative to check that the function is decreasing in order to satisfy the first condition
  • If the second condition is not satisfied and $ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty}b_n \neq 0 $, then we also know that $ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} (-1)^nb_n = DNE $ due to the alternating nature of the terms, and by the test for divergence then $ \sum (-1)^n b_n $ also diverges with $ a_n = (-1)^n b_n $

Alternating Series Estimation Theorem

If $ s $ is the sum of an alternating convergent series, then

$$ |R_n| = |S - S_n| \leq b_{n+1} $$

where $ R_n $ is the remainder/error.

Example

(a) How many terms of the series that’s convergent and alternating do we need to add up the approximate sum to have an error bound of 0.01 or less?

$$ \sum (-1)^{n-1} \frac{1}{n} $$

Using the alternating series estimation theorem, we have

$$ |R_n| \leq b_{n+1} \leq \frac{1}{n+1} $$

and because we want the error bound to be 0.01 or less, we set the remainder to be less than or equal to $ 0.01 $ as follows:

$$ \frac{1}{n+1} \leq \frac{1}{100} $$

solving for $ n $, we get $ n \geq 99 $, which means that we need at least 99 terms to have an erorr bound within $0.01$.
